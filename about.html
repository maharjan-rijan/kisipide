Introduction

Kisipide (Nepal Bhasa: किपुल्चा Kipulcha) is a Small Newari Village and an ancient city of Nepal. The Newars are the natives of Kipulcha (Kisipide) that is believed to be derived from Licchavi. It is located in the Kathmandu Valley 10 km south-west of the city of Kathmandu. Previously, It is in Mahadevsthan Villege Development Community Ward No – 1 and Now, became Chandragiri Municipality Ward No – 5. It is one of the most famous and religious places to visit. Jyapu’s are locals of Kipulcha (Kisipide) but nowadays we found Shrestha, Khadgi, Nakarmi and other non-Newars in this village. Kisipide village is situated below ta Daurali hill and Mathatirtha place of pilgrimage inside Kathmandu valley where big festival occurs during Mother’s Day.

Geographical Introduction

Kisipide (Nepal Bhasa: किपुल्चा Kipulcha) is smallest ancient city of Nepal located in the Kathmandu Valley which is 10 km South-west of the city. Chandragiri Municipality one of the biggest municipalities in Kathmandu containing 15 wards. Among these wards Kisipide is in ward no 5. This village is connected with Satungal in east, Thankot in west, Daurali hill in south and Balambu in North. According to the census 2068 record the total population of Chandragiri ward on 5 is 4,838 among them 2,427 were men and 2,411 were women.

Historical Introduction

Kisipide is an ancient Newari village. It is believed that this village was developed from Licchavi period which is proved from record written during Licchavi period. In the record it is found that Kisipide is named as Kispichingram (किचप्रिचिङग्राम). In Sanka Sambat 482 according to record of Licchavi period Kisipide is named as Sitatikatle Kispichingram (शीताटीकातले किचप्रिचिङग्राम). We can find different name of village from different ancient record such as: Kisipande, Kisiped, Kisipre, Kisper, Kisiprede, Kisipide etc. In middle age during the period of development of different village Kisipide is named as Hastinapur. In middle age record of Nepal Sambat 782 magh krishana 13 which is found in Mahadev Temple of Mathatirtha Kisipide is named as Kispichingram (किचप्रिचिङग्राम). Similarly, record of Nepal Sambat 806 it named as Kisipide.

Social Introduction

Kisipide is Newari village however some percentage of non-Newars also used to live in this village. Most of the villagers of this village involved in agricultural as their main occupation. In this ancient village there only one gate (Nepal Bhasa:ध्वाखां Dhwaka) to enter. During different festivals villagers use to go for worship in main temple of this village (Kalikabhirav temple) through same gate which is outside the village. In the Center of the village there is there is main street called Lachi(लाछि) and around the main street there are different another street. Outside the gate of the village there is a street named Dhoka pini (ध्वाखांपिने). Nani (ननि) another street of the village. From this street villagers start most important and biggest festival of the village because in this street there is God house of God Bhirav and Guthi house. To be united and to perform all development work of village peoples established guthi. Jyapus of this village divided guthi into different group such as: Biggest Guthi and Smallest Guthi. Similarly, Other group of this village also have their own Guthi. These Guthi is not only for Social and cultural festivals but also for different social works.

Cultural And Traditional Introduction

Newars yearly celebrate different festivals. This help to forward our tradition and culture which is identity of our forefathers. If we look our culture from outside then it give us historical importance only but if we look deeply then it give us scientific importance.
